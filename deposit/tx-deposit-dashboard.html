Deposit Competitiveness Score Methodology

How each bank's Competitiveness Score (0-100) is calculated to identify the most aggressive deposit competitors in the Texas community banking market.

Score Interpretation
0-34 Low
35-59 Moderate
60-79 Competitive
80+ Aggressive
Scoring Components (5 factors, 100 points max)
Cost of Funds 0-35 pts
What it measures: The bank's blended deposit cost from FDIC Call Report data (EINTEXP ÷ DEP).
How it works: Higher cost of funds = higher score. A bank paying 3%+ in blended deposit costs scores 35/35. Linear scale from 0%.
Why it matters: Banks with high cost of funds are paying depositors more — they're actively competing for deposits and will be Ranger Bank's primary competitors.
Deposit Growth Proxy 0-20 pts
What it measures: Interest expense as a percentage of total assets — a proxy for deposit-gathering intensity.
How it works: Higher ratio = more aggressive deposit pricing relative to bank size. Banks spending 3%+ of assets on interest expense score 20/20.
Why it matters: This normalizes for bank size. A small bank spending a high share of its asset base on interest expense is especially aggressive.
Asset Size Fit 0-15 pts
What it measures: Whether the bank falls in the $100M-$1B "sweet spot" for competitive deposit activity.
How it works: $100M-$1B = 15pts, $1B-$3B = 12pts, under $100M = 10pts, other = 5pts.
Why it matters: Mid-sized community banks are the most active competitors for local deposits — large enough to offer competitive products, small enough to need every deposit dollar.
Website Presence 0-10 pts
What it measures: Whether the bank has a verified website on file with the FDIC.
How it works: Website on file = 10pts, no website = 0pts.
Why it matters: Banks with websites are digitally active and more likely to publish competitive rates, making them visible competitors for online-savvy depositors.
Scraped Rate Data 0-20 pts
What it measures: Whether the bank has publicly advertised deposit rates that were successfully scraped.
How it works: Has scraped product rates = 20pts, no scraped data = 0pts.
Why it matters: Banks that prominently advertise rates are actively marketing their deposit products — the clearest signal of competitive intent.
Data Sources
FDIC BankFind API — Cost of funds, total deposits, total assets, interest expense, charter type, and website URLs for all active Texas community banks under $3B in assets.
Scraped Bank Websites — Advertised deposit product rates collected from 19 bank websites as of the most recent scrape date.
Limitations
• FDIC cost-of-funds is a lagging indicator from quarterly Call Reports — does not reflect real-time rate changes
• Scraped rates cover only 19 of 318 banks — most banks receive 0/20 for this component by default
• The score measures observable competitiveness, not actual deposit growth or market share
• Banks without FDIC financial data receive 0 for the cost-of-funds and growth proxy components
