Scoring Components (3 factors, 100 points max)
Low Average APY (Ease of Entry) 0-40 pts
What it measures: How low the average advertised rate is in the market.
How it works: Formula: (1 - avgAPY/5) × 40. A market averaging 0% APY scores 40/40; a market averaging 5% scores 0/40.
Why it matters: Markets with low existing rates are easiest for Ranger Bank to enter competitively — even a modest rate offer can be market-leading, attracting depositors without aggressive pricing.
Market Size (Bank Count) 0-30 pts
What it measures: How many banks with scraped rates operate in the market.
How it works: Formula: min(bankCount/5, 1) × 30. Markets with 5+ banks score 30/30; fewer banks scale proportionally.
Why it matters: Larger markets have more deposit volume available and more established demand. A market with many banks indicates healthy deposit activity worth competing for.
Product Variety 0-30 pts
What it measures: How many different deposit product types are offered in the market (CD, savings, money market, interest checking).
How it works: Formula: min(productTypeCount/4, 1) × 30. Markets with all 4 product types score 30/30.
Why it matters: Markets with diverse product offerings indicate mature deposit competition. These markets have customers who actively compare products — exactly the audience Ranger Bank can target.
Data Sources
FDIC BankFind API — Cost of funds (EINTEXP ÷ DEPI, annual interest expense over interest-bearing deposits), total deposits, interest-bearing deposits, total assets, charter type, and branch locations for all Texas community banks under $3B in assets.
Scraped Bank Websites — Advertised deposit product rates (CD, savings, money market, interest checking) collected from 19 bank websites. Refreshed periodically.
Federal Reserve (FRED) — Federal Funds effective rate for benchmark comparison.
Limitations
• Scraped rates cover only 19 of 318 TX banks — market scores are based on available data, not the full competitive field
• FDIC cost-of-funds is a blended average from quarterly call reports, not current advertised rates
• Market boundaries are based on FDIC-recorded city, not MSA or trade area definitions
• The score does not account for branch proximity, brand strength, or customer switching costs
• Rates change frequently — scraped data reflects a point-in-time snapshot
