Capital-Normalized Purchase Price — Methodology
Standardizes purchase price across targets by assuming each acquired bank is brought to a 10.0% Tier 1 leverage ratio post-close. This neutralizes the effect of excess (or deficient) capital so deal economics can be compared apples-to-apples.

Step 1: Required Equity = Total Assets × Target Leverage %
Step 2: Excess Equity = Current Equity − Required Equity (negative = capital injection needed)
Step 3: Effective Tangible Equity = TBVE − Excess Equity
Step 4: Normalized Price = Effective Tangible Equity × Multiple

Purchase Price Valuation

  • Tangible Book Value of Equity (TBVE) = Total Equity (EQTOT) − Total Intangible Assets (INTAN). INTAN already includes goodwill plus other intangibles; subtracting it produces the tangible book that an acquirer is actually paying for.
  • TBVE source: FDIC BankFind Financials API. Each bank's most recent quarterly call report is used (REPDTE field shown alongside).
  • Purchase price at multiple: Simple = TBVE × Multiple. Multiples 0.75x – 2.00x. Reference points: 0.85x distressed floor · 1.05x TX small-bank median · 1.25x healthy mid-cycle · 1.50x premium franchise · 1.85x top-tier (S&P Global Market Intelligence, Mercer Capital annual reviews).
  • Capital-Normalized Price: Standardizes for capital structure by recasting each bank to a target Tier 1 leverage (default 10.0%). Required Equity = Assets × Target Leverage %. Excess Capital = Current Equity − Required Equity. Effective Tangible Equity = TBVE − Excess Capital. Normalized Price = Effective Tangible Equity × Multiple. Banks with excess capital see a lower normalized price (paying for cash is suboptimal); under-capitalized banks see a higher normalized price (acquirer must inject capital at close).
  • Caveat: Multiples are directional. Actual deal pricing depends on diligence, regulatory approval, retention terms, earn-outs, and indemnification.

Shareholder dynamics — FR Y-6 access

  • Primary source for private banks: FR Y-6 Annual Report of Holding Companies — filed annually with the Federal Reserve by all top-tier U.S. bank holding companies. Item 4 ("List of Securities Holders") discloses every shareholder owning 5%+ of any class of voting securities including name, country, state, type of entity, and percentage owned.
  • Access: Public via the FFIEC National Information Center (NIC). Each target's holding company has a profile page (linked from the valuation table) where the FR Y-6 PDF can be downloaded.
  • Automation limitation: NIC uses CAPTCHA-based bot protection that blocks bulk automated parsing. The dashboard surfaces direct deep-links so users can retrieve filings via browser. For desk-level due diligence this works well; for institutional bulk research, FFIEC's commercial subscription feed is required.
  • Secondary source for public banks: SEC EDGAR DEF 14A proxies. The "Security Ownership of Certain Beneficial Owners and Management" table lists 5%+ holders and aggregate director/officer holdings. Only 2 of 95 targets are SEC-registered.
  • Coverage: 71 of 95 targets have a holding company on file (FR Y-6 retrievable). 2 are SEC-registered. 22 banks have no holding company on file — ownership for those requires direct disclosure during outreach.
  • Caveat: FR Y-6 reflects ownership as of filing date (typically March 31 of the year following the reporting year). Material ownership changes between filing and today may not be captured. Always confirm via direct outreach before negotiation.

Valuation considerations — auto-flags

  • Goodwill burden — if goodwill > 25% of equity, impairment risk warrants haircut
  • Excess capital — capital above the 10% normalization threshold transfers in the deal; price net of excess for true economic value
  • Capital shortfall — capital below threshold requires injection at close, reducing effective acquisition value
  • Multi-bank holdco — may require carve-out or HoldCo-level transaction structure
  • Low cost-of-funds franchise — bottom quartile CoF justifies premium multiple
  • Earnings under peer — ROA more than 20 bps below peer median warrants haircut
  • Asset growth outpacing equity — asset_cagr_5y > equity_cagr_5y by 5+ pp suggests leveraging up; verify credit quality
  • No holdco — no FR Y-6 available; ownership must be disclosed directly during outreach
