Methodology & Sources

Every benchmark on this dashboard traces to a specific public source. Data vintage and citations below.

Bank Opportunity Score (0-100) — proprietary composite
Components:
• Income level (25 pts) — based on Medscape average
• Payer mix attractiveness (20 pts) — derived from estimated commercial vs. Medicare/Medicaid mix
• Income stability (15 pts) — based on revenue volatility per specialty
• Capex / loan demand (15 pts) — moderate is best; high capex = financing opportunity but more leverage risk
• Demand outlook (10 pts) — BLS 10-year projected employment growth
• Overhead efficiency (10 pts) — lower overhead = higher score
• Default risk inverse (5 pts) — directional based on payer mix and industry data
Payer Mix Attractiveness Score (0-100)
Based on national specialty norms: Score = (Commercial × 1.0) + (Medicare × 0.5) + (Self-pay × 0.4) + (Other × 0.4) + (Medicaid × 0.1), rescaled to 100. Commercial pays ~150-200% of Medicare on average (Urban Institute 2017-2019). Medicaid typically pays 50-70% of Medicare. Self-pay carries collection risk but higher gross margin if collected.
Medscape Physician Compensation Report 2024 ↗
Vintage: Survey fielded Oct 2024 – Jan 2025; reflects calendar 2024 income.
Sample: 7,300+ physicians across 29+ specialties.
Caveat: Self-reported; full-time only; includes incentive bonuses.
Used for: Average compensation column for all specialties.
Doximity Physician Compensation Report 2025 ↗
Vintage: 2024 calendar-year compensation data; 37,000+ surveys Jan-Dec 2024.
Caveat: Includes profit sharing, bonuses; clinically active full-time only. Reports higher specialty averages than Medscape because it covers more procedural specialties.
Used for: Doximity comp column, top/bottom metro rankings.
BLS OEWS May 2024 ↗
Vintage: May 2024 (released April 2025).
Caveat: Covers W-2 employed physicians only; excludes self-employed practice owners. Wage = base + production bonus only (no equity/K-1 income). Best read as a floor for owner-physicians.
Used for: BLS W-2 Mean column. SOC codes 29-1211 through 29-1248.
MedPAC July 2024 Data Book ↗
Vintage: 2022/2023 Medicare claims data.
Used for: Medicare share inputs at the specialty group level. Note: precise per-specialty Medicare share requires aggregating the CMS PUF directly.
CMS Geographic Practice Cost Index (GPCI) 2024 ↗
Vintage: CY 2024 (effective Jan 1, 2024). Conversion factor: $32.7442.
Used for: Geographic adjustment factors in the Geographic View tab. Composite GAF = Work × 0.5089 + Practice Expense × 0.4484 + Malpractice × 0.0428.
Urban Institute — Commercial Markups over Medicare ↗
Vintage: 2017-2019 (structurally stable since).
Used for: Payer mix attractiveness calibration. Anesthesiology commercial rates ~330% of Medicare; orthopedics ~150%; family medicine ~110%; dermatology ~90% (lowest among major specialties).
MGMA Public Benchmarks ↗
Vintage: 2023-2024.
Caveat: Specialty-level overhead and Days-in-AR are paywalled in MGMA DataDive. Public benchmark: Days in A/R <40 (best practice). Overhead 60-70% of revenue (industry rule of thumb). The per-specialty overhead figures shown on this dashboard are derived from public industry sources, trade publications, and a single MGMA-disclosed figure (Gastroenterology = 54.2%). Treat them as planning estimates with ±5pp uncertainty; request borrower P&L for actuals.
Per-Specialty Payer Mix Estimates
Caveat: Public per-specialty payer-mix breakdowns are not freely published at this granularity. Estimates here are derived from (a) MedPAC age-group utilization curves, (b) AMA 2017 Patient-Mix Benchmark Survey, (c) Urban Institute markup analysis, and (d) practice-management trade publications. Treat as planning estimates with ±10pp uncertainty. Always request actual borrower payer mix from the practice CFO/manager for credit decisions.
Borrower Comparison — Threshold Logic
For each metric the borrower enters, the dashboard computes a percentile rank against the specialty distribution:
• Income: Green if ≥ 50th percentile, Yellow if 25-50th, Red if < 25th
• Overhead %: Green if ≤ specialty median, Yellow if up to +5pp, Red if > +5pp
• Days in A/R: Green if ≤ 40, Yellow if 40-55, Red if > 55
• Collection rate: Green if ≥ 95%, Yellow 90-95%, Red < 90%
• Payer mix: Computed score vs. specialty average payer mix score
Limitations & Caveats
• Data lag: Most physician compensation reports publish 12-18 months after the calendar year. Use as directional, not real-time.
• Sub-specialty granularity: Where shown, sub-specialty figures often come from smaller survey samples (sometimes N<100). Flag the warning in the UI.
• Survey vs. administrative data: Medscape and Doximity are survey-based (self-reported); BLS is administrative (employer-reported). They differ structurally — neither is "right."
• Equity / partnership distributions: Compensation figures generally do not include K-1 distributions, real estate income from medical office buildings, or equity from ASC ownership. Material for high earners.
• Geographic adjustment: GPCI applies to Medicare only, not commercial. Use as directional proxy for cost-of-living adjustment.
• Refresh schedule: Dashboard data is refreshed when source reports publish (annually for most). See refresh_data.py in the source repository.
